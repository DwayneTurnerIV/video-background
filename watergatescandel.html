On June 17, 1972, several people were caught on site of Democratic National Committee, located in the Watergate building in Washington, D.C. and were arrested. Now tresspassing would of be one thing that is viable for their arrests but they had been attempting to wiretap phones and steal secret documents but what makes this story even more shocking was that the burgulars were somehow connected to President Richard Nixon and his re-election campaign. Now many expert historians are unsure whether Nixon actually knew about this undercover operation before it happened however, he did not try to distance himself from the burglers as much as possible but he also when as far as to cover it up afterwards, raising money for the criminals to keep them quiet, trying to stop the FBI from digging deeper into the scandal, finding and destroying evidence as well as sacking staff members that were not co-operating. Nixon's days were finally numbered in August 1974. After his role in the Watergate conspiracy had finally come to light as well as other failures during his time in office including the very controversial Vietnam war, the president resigned.
